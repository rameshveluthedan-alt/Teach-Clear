### Chapter: Prime Time

## Multiples

Multiples are numbers obtained by multiplying a number.

Example:
Multiples of 3 → 3, 6, 9, 12...

---

## Common Multiples

Common multiples are shared multiples of two numbers.

Example:
Multiples of 3 → 3, 6, 9...
Multiples of 5 → 5, 10, 15...
Common multiple → 15

---

## Factors

Factors are numbers that divide another number exactly.

Example:
Factors of 24 → 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12, 24

---

## Common Factors

Numbers that are factors of two or more numbers.

Example:
Factors of 14 → 1, 2, 7, 14  
Factors of 36 → 1, 2, 3, 4, 6...  
Common factors → 1, 2

---

## Prime Numbers

A prime number has exactly two factors:
- 1 and itself

Examples:
2, 3, 5, 7, 11...

---

## Composite Numbers

Numbers with more than two factors.

Examples:
4, 6, 8, 9...

---

## Key Takeaways

- Multiples → repeated addition
- Factors → exact division
- Prime numbers → only two factors
- Common factors and multiples help solve problems
