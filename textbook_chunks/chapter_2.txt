### Chapter: Lines and Angles

## Basic Concepts

Geometry studies shapes and their properties.

Key elements:
- Point
- Line
- Line segment
- Ray
- Angle

---

## Point

A point represents an exact location.

Properties:
- No length, breadth, or height

Examples:
- Tip of a pencil
- Dot on paper

---

## Line Segment

A line segment:
- Has two endpoints
- Is the shortest distance between two points

---

## Line

A line:
- Extends infinitely in both directions
- Has no endpoints

---

## Ray

A ray:
- Starts at one point
- Extends infinitely in one direction

---

## Angle

An angle is formed by two rays with a common starting point.

Parts:
- Vertex (common point)
- Arms (two rays)

---

## Size of an Angle

Angle size depends on rotation.

More rotation → larger angle

---

## Types of Angles

- Acute angle: less than 90°
- Right angle: exactly 90°
- Obtuse angle: between 90° and 180°
- Straight angle: 180°

---

## Measuring Angles

Angles are measured in degrees (°).

- Full turn = 360°
- Straight angle = 180°
- Right angle = 90°

Tool used:
- Protractor

---

## Comparing Angles

Angles can be compared by:
- Superimposition (placing one over another)
- Rotation comparison

---

## Key Takeaways

- Angles measure rotation
- Geometry builds from simple concepts
- Angles are classified based on size
