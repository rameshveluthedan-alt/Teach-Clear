### Chapter: Data Handling and Presentation

## What is Data?
Data is a collection of facts, numbers, observations, or information.

Examples:
- Favourite games of students
- Heights or weights of students
- Survey responses

---

## Collecting Data

To answer questions like “most popular game”, we must:
- Ask people (survey)
- Record responses

---

## Organising Data

Raw data is difficult to interpret.

We organise data using:
- Lists
- Tables
- Tally marks

---

## Tally Marks

Tally marks help count easily:
- Every 5th mark crosses previous four
- Helps quick counting

---

## Frequency

The number of times a value appears is called frequency.

Example:
- 6 students like jalebi → frequency = 6

---

## Arranging Data

Data can be arranged:
- Ascending order
- Descending order

Helps identify:
- smallest value
- largest value
- patterns

---

## Pictographs

Pictographs represent data using symbols or pictures.

Features:
- Easy to understand
- Visual comparison
- Each symbol represents a number

---

## Key Takeaways

- Data helps answer real-world questions
- Organisation improves understanding
- Visual representation makes interpretation easier
