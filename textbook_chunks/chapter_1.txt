### Chapter: Patterns in Mathematics

## What is Mathematics?
Mathematics is the study of patterns and understanding why those patterns exist.

Patterns are found everywhere:
- Nature
- Daily activities (shopping, cooking, games)
- Technology and science

Mathematics helps explain these patterns and apply them in real life.

---

## Patterns in Numbers

Number patterns include sequences such as:

- Counting numbers: 1, 2, 3, 4...
- Odd numbers: 1, 3, 5, 7...
- Even numbers: 2, 4, 6, 8...
- Square numbers: 1, 4, 9, 16...
- Cube numbers: 1, 8, 27...
- Triangular numbers: 1, 3, 6, 10...

Number theory studies patterns in numbers.

---

## Visualising Number Patterns

Number patterns can be shown using pictures.

Examples:
- Square numbers → arranged in square shapes
- Triangular numbers → arranged in triangle shapes

Visualisation helps understand concepts better.

---

## Relations Among Number Patterns

Patterns can be related.

Example:
Sum of odd numbers:
1 = 1  
1 + 3 = 4  
1 + 3 + 5 = 9  
1 + 3 + 5 + 7 = 16  

Observation:
→ Sum of first n odd numbers = n² (square numbers)

---

## Patterns in Shapes

Patterns also exist in shapes (geometry).

Examples:
- Triangles
- Squares
- Polygons
- Graphs

---

## Relation Between Shapes and Numbers

Example:
- Number of sides of polygons: 3, 4, 5, 6...

Shape patterns often connect with number patterns.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Mathematics is about patterns and explanations
- Number sequences are fundamental
- Visualisation helps understanding
- Shape and number patterns are connected
